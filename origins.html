What is Language?

In most dictionaries, language is defined as "a system of conventional spoken, manual, or written symbols by means of which human beings, as members of a social group and participants in its culture, express themselves. The functions of language include communication, the expression of identity, play, imaginative expression, and emotional release." (Britcanica.com)

However, linguists all over the world cannot agree on one defintion of language. Language can consists of various elements, not just syntax, grammar, pragmatics, etc. Moreover, defining language inherently probes the question: does language exist in non-human species? Some believe language is unique to humans, while others cite Alex the parrot and other animals to prove that language is not uniquely human.

Despite the lack of consensus, language as humans know it consists of various components. Below, is a brief explanation of what constitutes human langauge. While this definition seems to capture the essence of language as we know it today, it does not reflect the specific levels of language and its impact on human evolution and development.





Levels of Language

There are five main levels of language:

  1. Phonetics/Phonology: Describes the level of sounds.
  2. Morphology: Describes how the sounds come together to make worlds. Morphology also deals with how words change (sound wise) when new endings are added (sufixes and prefixes)
  3. Syntax: Discusses how words come together to form coherent sentences.
  4. Pragmatics: Discusses how these sentences come together to form coherent and efficent language. Pragmatics deals with how language is used in different scenarios.
